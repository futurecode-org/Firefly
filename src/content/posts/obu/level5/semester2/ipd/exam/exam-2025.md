## Innovative Product Development - Class Test

1. **Who is primarily responsible for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Scrum Team?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. The Scrum Master  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The Developers  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. The Product Owner  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. The Project Manager  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. The Product Owner]



2. **You are managing a project team that currently has 6 members, including yourself. Two new members are scheduled to join next week.** <br>**How many communication channels will exist after the new members join?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. 15  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. 21  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. 28  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. 36  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. 28]



3. **What is the main purpose of the Sprint Review?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. For the Developers to demonstrate their work and get feedback.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. To inspect the outcome of the Sprint and determine future adaptations.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. For the Scrum Master to assess the performance of the Developers.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. To plan the work for the next Sprint.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. To inspect the outcome of the Sprint and determine future adaptations.]



4. **If the Developers realize they have selected too much work for a Sprint, what is the most appropriate first course of action?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Ask the Scrum Master to cancel the Sprint.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Work overtime to finish all the selected items.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Collaborate with the Product Owner to adjust the scope of the Sprint Backlog.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Remove items from the Sprint Backlog without consulting anyone.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. Collaborate with the Product Owner to adjust the scope of the Sprint Backlog.]



5. **Which Scrum accountability is focused on ensuring the Scrum framework is understood and enacted?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Product Owner  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Developers  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Scrum Master  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Team Lead  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. Scrum Master]



6. **During the Sprint Planning, the Developers select items from the Product Backlog to include in the Sprint Backlog. What else must they create during this event?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. A detailed plan for how they will build the Increment.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The Definition of Done.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. The Product Goal.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. User acceptance test cases.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[A. A detailed plan for how they will build the Increment.]



7. **The primary goal of the Sprint Retrospective is to:**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Plan work for the next Sprint.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Identify potential improvements in process, tools, and collaboration for future Sprints.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Allow stakeholders to provide feedback on the product.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. Identify potential improvements in process, tools, and collaboration for future Sprints.]



8. **The Definition of Done can only be updated between Sprints, not during a Sprint.**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. True  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. False  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. False]



9. **The Definition of Done helps ensure _______________.**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. The Product Owner is satisfied  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The Sprint Goal is met  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Transparency regarding the state of the Increment  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. The team's velocity increases  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[C. Transparency regarding the state of the Increment]



10. **Scrum is based on which theory? Product Backlog refinement is the act of:**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Finalizing the Sprint Backlog during Sprint Planning.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Adding detail, estimates, and order to Product Backlog items.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Reviewing the completed Increment with stakeholders.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Removing technical debt from the codebase.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. Adding detail, estimates, and order to Product Backlog items.]



11. **What is the commitment associated with the Product Backlog?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. The Sprint Goal  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The Definition of Done  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. The Increment  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. The Product Goal  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[D. The Product Goal]



12. **The Sprint Goal is created during which Scrum event?**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Daily Scrum  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Sprint Review  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Sprint Retrospective  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Sprint Planning  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[D. Sprint Planning]



13. **The Product Backlog is considered complete when all initially conceived items are done.**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. True  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. False  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. False]



14. **Return on Investment (ROI) calculation typically helps in: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Prioritizing features based on potential value.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Justifying project funding and continuation.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Measuring the exact lines of code delivered.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Comparing the profitability of different projects or features.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[A. Prioritizing features based on potential value.<br>B. Justifying project funding and continuation.<br>D. Comparing the profitability of different projects or features.]



15. **Which of the following are common Agile estimation techniques? (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Planning Poker  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. T-Shirt Sizing  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Function Point Analysis  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Guessing randomly  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[A. Planning Poker<br>B. T-Shirt Sizing]



16. **Technical debt, if not managed, typically leads to: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Increased cost of future changes.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Improved software quality.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Slower development velocity over time.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Higher team morale.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[A. Increased cost of future changes.<br>C. Slower development velocity over time.]



17. **Velocity in Scrum is primarily used for: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Comparing the performance of different teams.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Helping the Developers forecast how much work they can complete in future Sprints.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Determining individual developer productivity.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Facilitating planning and release forecasting.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. Helping the Developers forecast how much work they can complete in future Sprints.<br>D. Facilitating planning and release forecasting.]



18. **A Burndown chart primarily shows: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. The total scope of the project.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The amount of work remaining over time.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. The work completed over time.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Progress towards the Sprint Goal or release.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. The amount of work remaining over time.<br>D. Progress towards the Sprint Goal or release.]



19. **Refactoring is done primarily to: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Add new functionality to the software.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. Improve the internal structure or design of the code without changing its external behaviour.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. Fix bugs discovered during testing.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. Make the code easier to understand and maintain.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[B. Improve the internal structure or design of the code without changing its external behaviour.<br>D. Make the code easier to understand and maintain.]



20. **A team's velocity might decrease unexpectedly if: (Select all that apply)**

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. Team members go on vacation or leave the team.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B. The Definition of Done becomes more stringent.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C. The team encounters unforeseen technical challenges.  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D. The Product Owner provides clear requirements.  

> [!CHECK] Reference Answer
> :spoiler[A. Team members go on vacation or leave the team.<br>B. The Definition of Done becomes more stringent.<br>C. The team encounters unforeseen technical challenges.]
