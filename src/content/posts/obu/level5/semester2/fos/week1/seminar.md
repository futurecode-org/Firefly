## Ethical hacking

### What is hacking

- Hacking involves exploiting weaknesses in computer networks to gain unauthorized access to information.
- A hacker is someone who attempts to penetrate computer systems. 
- <span style="color: blue">It is a misconception that hacking is always wrong</span>. 

### Types of Hackers

- There are <span style="color: red">three</span> types of hackers:
    - <span style="color: blue">Black Hat Hackers:</span> Individuals who illegally hack systems for financial gain. Criminal intent, stealing data, deploying malware, financial fraud etc 
        - <span style="color: red">ILLEGAL</span>
    - <span style="color: blue">White Hat Hackers (Ethical Hackers):</span> These hackers exploit vulnerabilities with permission to help organizations identify security weaknesses.
        - <span style="color: green">APPROVED</span>
    - <span style="color: blue">Gray Hat Hackers:</span> They find and report vulnerabilities without the owner's consent and may sometimes request payment for these discoveries. A mix of black and white hat hacking.
        - <span style="color: orange">NOT APPROVED</span>

## Case Study of Ethical Hacking

<span style="color: red">Why is Ethical Hacking Necessary?</span>

John runs a trading company providing online trading services with customer investments. As his business expanded, <mark>a hacker targeted the company's servers</mark>, stealing credentials of significant accounts and demanding a lump-sum ransom in exchange. 

John underestimated the threat and did not pay the ransom. Consequently, the hacker withdrew money from various customer accounts, leaving John liable for the losses. This incident cost John not only a substantial amount of money but also the trust of his customers.
![](img/Pasted_image_20260312173159.png)

---

After the incident, John reflected deeply on **what went wrong** with the security infrastructure of his company. 

He wished he had someone who could <mark>simulate an attack to assess system vulnerabilities</mark> before actual hacker penetration. 

Realizing the need, **he employed Dav, an ethical hacker**, to perform this role. Dav, skilled in mimicking hacker techniques, identified and solved several security loopholes.

> Hiring an ethical hacker not only protected his customers from future attacks but also **increased the company’s productivity and safeguarded its reputation**.

![](img/Pasted_image_20260312173334.png)

## Phases of Ethical Hacking Continue...

<span style="color: red">Ethical hacking is distributed into six different phases.</span>
![](img/Pasted_image_20260312173441.png)
<span style="color: blue">Six different phases of ethical hacking</span>

---

- Ethical hacking is divided into six distinct phases:
    1. <span style="color: blue">Reconnaissance</span>: Dav first gathers all necessary information about the organization’s systems he intends to test. Common tools used in this phase include <i><b><span style="color: red">NMAP</span></b></i> and <i><b><span style="color: red">hping</span></b></i>.
    2. <span style="color: blue">Scanning</span>: Using tools like <i><b><span style="color: red">NMAP</span></b></i> and <i><b><span style="color: red">xnexpose</span></b></i>, Dav searches for vulnerabilities in the target system.
    3. <span style="color: blue">Gaining Access</span>: Dav attempts to exploit the identified vulnerabilities to access the organization’s network.
    4. <span style="color: blue">Maintaining Access</span>: To simulate sustained network control, Dav installs backdoors using tools like <i><b><span style="color: red">Metasploit</span></b></i>.
    5. <span style="color: blue">Clearing Tracks</span>: Dav carefully covers his tracks to prevent detection, ensuring no residual signs or traces of intrusion remain.
    6. <span style="color: blue">Reporting</span>: In the final phase, Dav prepares a comprehensive report summarizing the attack, detailing vulnerabilities found, tools used, and the overall success rate of the simulation.

![](img/Pasted_image_20260312173830.png)
These phases help an ethical hacker like Dav to thoroughly assess and improve an organization’s security.

---

<span style="color: red">Do you all agree that Dav is an asset to any organization?</span>

If you aspire to become an ethical hacker like Dav, here are some skills you need to acquire:
- <span style="color: blue">Operating Environments</span>: Proficiency in Windows, Linux, UNIX, and MAC is crucial.
- <span style="color: blue">Programming Languages</span>: A solid understanding of HTML, PHP, Python, SQL, and JavaScript is necessary.
- <span style="color: blue">Networking</span>: Networking is the base of ethical hacking. Therefore, you should be good at networking.
- <span style="color: blue">Legal Knowledge</span>: Ethical hackers must be well-versed in security laws to avoid misusing their abilities.
- <span style="color: blue">Certification</span>: Obtaining a global certification in ethical hacking is essential to secure a position like Dav’s.

These skills equip you to protect organizations and enhance their security measures effectively.
